Damage Symptoms

Stink bugs damage small to medium-sized cotton bolls. Feeding damage introduces hard lock and boll rot pathogens into the boll, causing spotting and “warts” on the inner boll wall surface. A wart is the callous growth response to stink bug feeding. This initial damage often takes place within the first 24-48 hours after feeding. Subsequently, the new white developing lint may first turn yellow, then brownish in the area of the feeding site, or even throughout the boll.

Early boll damage most closely associated with cotton yield and quality losses are stained lint and internal boll warts. If a boll has either type of damage, however small, that boll is counted as damaged. External boll feeding spots caused by stink bugs and the tiny “pin prick” dark spot symptoms on the inner boll wall surface are not counted as an injured boll. Scouts should crush open and examine quarter-sized bolls (0.9 to 1.1 inches in diameter), looking for internal boll warts or stained lint.
